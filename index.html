South Asia Country-level Occupations and Skills Catalogs & Interactive Dashboards

The Country-level Occupations and Skills Catalogs (COSCs) are data-driven reference catalogs that describe the structure of employment in five priority sectors across South Asia. They were constructed using nationally representative labor force surveys or household surveys, ensuring that the analysis reflects each country’s employment structure.

For each country, the COSC identifies the occupations most commonly found within each priority sector and profiles them using a consistent set of labor market indicators. These include each occupation’s share of sector employment, the typical education level of workers, average wages, and the prevalence of self-employment, unpaid work, and informality.

Together, the COSCs provide a practical evidence base for understanding who works in priority sectors, what types of jobs are most common, and how employment conditions vary across occupations and countries.

U.S.A. Occupations and Skills Catalog & Interactive Dashboard

The U.S.A. Occupations and Skills Catalog was developed using data from O*NET and the Occupational Outlook Handbook to enrich the country-level occupations and skills catalogs with reference information on tasks, skills, and entry-level education and training pathways in a high-income economy.

This provides a structured starting point for understanding what different occupations typically involve in terms of tasks, the skills they require, and the education or training pathways. In this way, the U.S.A. Occupations and Skills Catalog offers a reference framework from a high-income economy that can help inform the use of the country-level catalogs for some applications.
